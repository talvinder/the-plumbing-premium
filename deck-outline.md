## Slide 2 — The Pattern

**Title:** Every Paradigm Shift Produces the Same Investing Mistake

**Key message:** Capital floods the application layer first, then discovers that infrastructure compounds while applications commoditize.

**Bullets:**
- First wave: investors fund apps built on top of the new platform
- Second wave: infrastructure companies pull ahead — higher margins, wider moats, stickier contracts
- This is not hindsight. The pattern is predictable and repeating right now.

**Visual description:** Simple two-column layout — "First Wave" vs "Second Wave" — with arrows showing where value accumulates over time.

**Speaker notes:** The mistake is not funding applications. The mistake is assuming applications hold their value. Once the platform matures, application logic becomes interchangeable. Infrastructure does not. This is the Plumbing Premium.

## Slide 3 — Historical Proof: Cloud Paradigm

**Title:** Cloud: AWS Won, Not the Apps Built on It

**Key message:** The web application boom created enormous value, but the compounding accrued to Amazon Web Services, not to the applications it hosted.

**Bullets:**
- 2005–2010: Investor capital chases web apps (social networks, SaaS tools, e-commerce)
- AWS launched 2006, quietly
- By 2023: AWS generates ~$90B revenue, ~30% operating margin
- Most web apps from that era are gone. AWS is the most profitable cloud business on Earth.

**Visual description:** Timeline graphic. AWS launch marked as a small dot in 2006, growing into a dominant line. App-layer companies shown as scattered dots with high attrition.

**Speaker notes:** Nobody pitched AWS as the big bet in 2006. Investors wanted the apps. AWS was the supporting cast. Thirteen years later, the supporting cast is the most valuable infrastructure business in history.

**Footer:** The Plumbing Premium — talvinder.com

## Slide 4 — Historical Proof: Payments Paradigm

**Title:** Payments: Stripe Compounded, Fintech Apps Churned

**Key message:** The fintech application layer produced many startups and few durable winners; Stripe built the rails and captured disproportionate value.

**Bullets:**
- 2010–2015: Investor capital floods fintech apps (lending, wallets, neobanks, PFM tools)
- Stripe launched 2010, focused on developer-first payment APIs
- Stripe valuation peak: ~$95B (2021); profitable, sticky, expanding into adjacent infrastructure
- Many fintech app-layer companies pivoted, merged, or failed

**Visual description:** Side-by-side comparison. Left: logos of notable fintech app failures/pivots. Right: Stripe revenue growth curve.

**Speaker notes:** Stripe did not try to build the best consumer fintech app. It built the plumbing. Every fintech app that launched needed Stripe or something like it. That is the position that compounds.

## Slide 6 — Why Infrastructure Wins

**Title:** Apps Commoditize. Infrastructure Compounds.

**Key message:** Application-layer differentiation erodes as model quality converges; infrastructure differentiation grows because switching costs increase with scale.

**Bullets:**
- AI app moat: prompt tuning, UX, distribution — all replicable
- AI infra moat: latency SLAs, multi-model routing, cold-start optimization, pricing at scale
- Every customer who scales on your infra is harder to move than the one who just signed up
- Infrastructure is sold to builders, not end users — shorter sales cycles, higher retention

**Visual description:** Two-axis chart. X-axis: time/maturity. Y-axis: competitive differentiation. Application layer curve slopes down. Infrastructure layer curve slopes up.

**Speaker notes:** The commoditization of AI application logic is already underway. Model quality is converging. Prompting strategies are public knowledge. The thing that does not commoditize is reliable, low-latency, cost-efficient model serving at scale. That is infrastructure.

**Footer:** The Plumbing Premium — talvinder.com

## Slide 7 — The Numbers: Revenue Multiples

**Title:** The Market Prices Infrastructure at a Premium

**Key message:** Infrastructure companies trade at higher revenue multiples than application-layer companies in the same paradigm.

**Bullets:**
- Modal Labs implied ARR multiple: ~22x (based on $1.1B valuation / ~$50M ARR)
- Fireworks AI implied multiple: ~16x+ (based on $4B valuation)
- Typical AI application SaaS: 5-8x ARR
- The premium reflects switching cost, margin profile, and scalability

**Visual description:** Bar chart comparing revenue multiples. Infrastructure companies grouped on the left (taller bars). Application-layer companies grouped on the right (shorter bars).

**Speaker notes:** Revenue multiples are imprecise proxies, but the direction is consistent. Markets assign a premium to infrastructure because the growth compounding mechanism is understood — more customers means more volume means more pricing power, not more churn.

## Slide 8 — What the Plumbing Layer Looks Like

**Title:** The Four Layers of AI Runtime Infrastructure

**Key message:** The AI plumbing layer is a stack of four distinct capability areas, each with its own market forming.

**Bullets:**
- **Inference:** Model serving, GPU orchestration, cold-start reduction (Modal, Baseten, Fireworks)
- **Orchestration:** Multi-model routing, agent coordination, workflow state (LangChain infra, LlamaIndex)
- **Observability:** Tracing, latency tracking, cost attribution, failure analysis (Helicone, Langfuse)
- **Evaluation:** Automated quality measurement, regression detection (Braintrust, Patronus)

**Visual description:** Vertical stack diagram. Bottom layer: inference. Above it: orchestration. Above it: observability. Top: evaluation. Each layer labeled with example companies.

**Speaker notes:** Each of these four layers is a distinct business with distinct customers and distinct moats. Together they form the complete runtime infrastructure stack.

## Slide 10 — The Investment Thesis

**Title:** Where Capital Should Flow

**Key message:** Infrastructure is the asymmetric bet in AI — earlier in the cycle than it appears, more durable than application bets.

**Bullets:**
- Application bets: high volume, high attrition, winner-take-most dynamics
- Infrastructure bets: lower volume, higher retention, compounding margins
- The asymmetry: infra companies serve all AI apps — they do not need to pick winners
- Timing: the window for early-stage infrastructure bets in AI is narrowing, not closed

**Visual description:** 2x2 matrix. X-axis: durability of moat. Y-axis: current capital competition. Infrastructure sits in the high-moat quadrant.

**Speaker notes:** The uncomfortable truth for application-layer investors is that their portfolio companies are customers of the infrastructure companies. Every dollar of application-layer ARR that scales requires more infrastructure spend.

**Footer:** The Plumbing Premium — talvinder.com

## Slide 11 — What This Means for Builders

**Title:** Build on Plumbing, or Build the Plumbing

**Key message:** Your strategic position depends on which side of the infrastructure layer you sit on.

**Bullets:**
- **Building an AI application:** Choose infra vendors on switching cost and SLA, not just price. Lock-in cuts both ways.
- **Building AI infrastructure:** Your moat is operational excellence, not product features. Distribution is developer-led.
- **Both:** Understand where in the stack your margin actually lives.

**Visual description:** Split layout. Left panel: "Application builder" checklist. Right panel: "Infrastructure builder" checklist.

**Speaker notes:** The worst position is building an application that depends on infrastructure you do not control and cannot afford to switch. The best position is building infrastructure that becomes the default choice for builders in your layer.

## Slide 12 — Summary

**Title:** The Plumbing Premium: One Slide

**Key message:** Infrastructure systematically outperforms applications once the market matures. This is not a hypothesis — it is a pattern.

**Bullets:**
- The pattern: capital floods apps first, value accumulates in infrastructure second
- The proof: AWS vs web apps; Stripe vs fintech apps; AI infra vs AI apps
- The signal: Modal ($1.1B), Baseten ($2.15B), Fireworks ($4B) — all infra layer
- The insight: apps commoditize, infra compounds
- The action: find your position on the stack

**Speaker notes:** The Plumbing Premium is not a prediction. It is a pattern. The builders and investors who recognize it early enough to act are the ones who compound alongside the infrastructure layer.
